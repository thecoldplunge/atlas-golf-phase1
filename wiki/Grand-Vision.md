# INTERGALACTIC GOLF TOUR — Grand Vision

## The One-Line Vision

A galaxy-spanning golf RPG where every stroke is a financial decision, every tournament is a survival gauntlet, and the path from your parents' couch on Earth to the top of the Intergalactic Golf Tour is brutal, expensive, and entirely up to you.

---

## The Fantasy

You're a kid with a golf swing and not much else. Somewhere out there — across fold-jumps and star systems — Voss champions are bombing 400-yard drives, Rill grandmasters are sinking impossible putts, and Paxi wind-readers are carving shots through crosswinds that would ground a ship. The Intergalactic Golf Tour is the most prestigious sporting organization in known space. No human has ever won the Grand Slam.

You're going to try.

But first you need to make rent.

---

## The Core Loop: Golf as Survival

This isn't a career mode where the money is a scoreboard. **The money is the game.**

Every week, the meter runs. Lodging costs money. Food costs money. Fold-jumps between star systems cost a fortune. Tournament entry fees, equipment repairs, coach sessions, practice range time — all of it bleeds from your account. Miss a cut and you don't just lose ranking points; you lose the ability to afford the next flight. String together two bad weeks and you're stranded on a foreign world, watching the tour depart without you.

**The loop:** Play well → earn prize money → afford travel + lodging + better gear → play in bigger events → earn more → climb. Or: play poorly → go broke → scramble for side income → claw your way back.

**Home base is your safety net and your trap.** Your parents' house on Earth is free to stay at. Free food. Free bed. But living at home crushes your focus stat — you can't train like a pro while your mom is yelling up the stairs about dinner. Renting an apartment costs money but restores your edge. Buying a house on Earth is a milestone. Buying a second home on an alien world is a flex. The housing system is progression made tangible.

---

## The Career Ladder

Nobody starts on the IGT.

1. **Open tournaments** (amateur, pay-to-enter, anyone welcome) → place high enough to qualify for an Earth Tour event.
2. **Earth Tour events** → place high enough there to earn an **Earth Tour Card**.
3. **Earth Tour** → dominate to get invited to an international tournament (off-world, the big leagues).
4. **International tournaments** → place high enough there to earn your **IGT Tour Card** — the real one, the galactic one.

Miss the cutoff? You wait. Next invite cycle. Which means more weeks of lodging you can barely afford.

Once you're on the IGT, **staying there is its own war.** Drop below ranking thresholds and you lose your card, your sponsors drop you, and you're grinding open tournaments again — except now you've got a mortgage.

The four **Majors** — **The Aeris Open**, **The Keldaran Masters**, **The Rill Invitational**, and **The Sol Classic** — are the summit. No human has held all four. That's the **Grand Slam**. That's the endgame.

## The Stats That Define You

Every golfer (player and NPC) has nine skills, each rated 0–100:

**Power · Accuracy · Touch · Spin Control · Putting · Composure · Course Management** (plus Focus and Recovery in the mental bucket)

Your stats shape every shot outcome. They also shape how you experience the game — a high-Composure player laughs off a bad break; a low-Composure player tilts and starts missing fairways.

Stats improve through paid training: lessons with coaches (for technique stats), speed training (for power), putting labs, mental coaches for focus/composure. But paying isn't enough. **Every training session triggers a mini-game** — nail it and you get the full stat gain; flub it and you paid for half a lesson. Money and skill both matter.

## When You're Broke: The Caddie Mini-Game

Run out of fold credits on a strange planet? You can always caddie.

You pick up a bag for a pro who needs help. The course situation appears: ball lie, distance to pin, wind, elevation, hazards. You know your player's stats and their bag. You call the shot — which club, which line, what to watch out for. Nail the read and your pro strikes it close; the tip rolls in. Misread the wind, hand them the wrong iron, tell them to lay up when they should've gone for it — base rate, no tip, maybe a scolding.

Reading putts is its own version: slope, grain, speed, break. Get it right and your player drains it. Get it wrong and they three-putt and you hear about it.

Good caddies get **requested back by pros** — recurring gigs with better rates. It's also how you learn the game: caddying forces you to see shots through another player's stats, which makes you a smarter golfer yourself.

## The Gambling Underlayer

The official Tour is just the surface. Everywhere you travel, there's another game running.

**Skins matches** in the parking lot behind the pro shop. **Scramble teams** cobbled together from whoever's broke that week. **Nassau side-bets** on the driving range. **Underground exhibition matches** organized by fixers and bookmakers in private fold-yachts. This is where you go when you need money fast and don't care who you're playing with. It's also where you can lose everything in an afternoon.

Some of the most dangerous — and profitable — golfers in the galaxy never appear on the IGT leaderboard. They live in the underlayer. Some of them want your money. Some of them want your loyalty. Some of them, if you're good enough, will want to play you.

---

## Sponsorships: Blessing and Leash

Finish well on tour and sponsors come calling.

**Apparel sponsors** (hats, shirts, pants, shoes) pay you a per-tournament appearance fee and provide free branded gear — which your character actually wears in-game, swapping your homemade fit for polished corporate drip. Your fit becomes a walking billboard.

**Equipment sponsors** (only available after making the IGT) loan you premium clubs. These are **rentals**, not gifts. Perform well and they let you keep using them. Drop below their ranking requirement and they yank the clubs back — you're stuck with whatever you owned before, which is usually worse.

The **best clubs in the galaxy cannot be sponsored.** They must be bought. And they are staggeringly expensive. Earning enough to buy a top-tier driver outright is a mid-career milestone.

Sponsors also come with obligations: media days, promotional appearances, branded press conferences. Some sponsors have **rivalries** with each other and won't sign you if you're already with their enemy. **Sponsor politics is a meta-game on top of the tour.**

## The World Map & Course Map

### Galactic map

Travel between star systems, planets, and space stations. Every fold-jump has a cost. Plan your tour schedule like a chess game — which events to fly to, which to skip, when to route through a cheap layover to save credits.

### Planetary / course map

Arrive at a planet and explore its tour venue. Every course has:

- **The clubhouse** — check-in, schedule, leaderboard.
- **The pro shop** — clubs, apparel, accessories, local specialty items. Every planet has unique gear.
- **The driving range & practice green** — pay to practice; stat buffs for the event.
- **The caddie hall** — pick up caddie gigs when broke.
- **The back lot** — where the skins matches, scrambles, and side-bet games live. Seedy, profitable, risky.
- **Lodging options** — cheap motel up through luxury suite. Each affects how rested you are for the event.
- **Local restaurants** — food costs money; skipping meals affects stats.

## Character Customization

Your character is a structured equipment system — every slot visibly changes your appearance in-game:

| Slot | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| **Head** | Hats, visors, helmets, ceremonial pieces |
| **Torso** | Polos, vests, jackets, sponsor-branded tops |
| **Legs** | Trousers, shorts, skorts, compression wear |
| **Feet** | Golf spikes, specialty terrain footwear |
| **Hands** | Gloves, tech gauntlets, grip wraps |
| **Accessories** | Sunglasses, watches, jewelry, pet mascots, belts |
| **Bag** | Golf bag cosmetics and capacity upgrades |
| **Clubs** (14 slots) | Driver, woods, hybrids, irons, wedges, putter |

Apparel is **cosmetic + light stat-influence** (a good hat might add +1 to focus in high-sun conditions). Clubs are the real gear game — each club has its own stats (distance range, forgiveness, spin profile, durability) and must be matched to your swing and the course conditions. Club selection before a round is a real strategic decision.

**Sponsor gear overrides your wardrobe while you're under contract** — lose the sponsor, lose the fit.

## Property & Long-Term Goals

Money isn't just for survival. Once you're winning, it becomes legacy.

- **Rent an apartment on Earth** — a small but real upgrade from your parents' place. Focus restored.
- **Buy a house on Earth** — a permanent home base, customizable, zero weekly lodging bleed.
- **Buy a second home off-world** — a pied-à-terre near a major tour stop. Skip lodging costs for every tournament in that system. A serious flex.
- **Luxury properties on prestige planets** — status symbols that unlock sponsor tiers, VIP tour access, and social events where the deepest-pocketed fans and sponsors mingle.

**Properties are the tangible measure of how far you've climbed.**

## Why This Works

Everything compounds. A good tournament pays for next week's travel, which gets you to a bigger event, which attracts a sponsor, which gives you better clubs, which lets you win more. A bad tournament starts the opposite spiral — and the caddie mini-game plus the bet underlayer exist so you always have a way to fight back, but at the cost of your time and your pride.

Every stat matters because every stat costs money to improve. Every club matters because every club costs money to buy. Every travel decision matters because every fold-jump costs money to make. The game is a series of tight, interlocking economic decisions where the right answer is almost never obvious, and the consequences always follow you.

**The player is never the hero by default.** Humans are underdogs. The Voss are stronger, the Rill are more precise, the Paxi have better touch. You're a generalist from the newest spacefaring species, playing a game older than your civilization, against opponents who've been training for it longer than your homeworld has had fold technology. Every stroke you win, you've earned.

## The Promise

You start the game broke, sleeping in your childhood bedroom, playing a municipal course on Earth in hand-me-down spikes and a polo with a peeling logo. Your focus is shot because your dad won't stop asking when you're getting a real job.

The endgame is you, hoisting a trophy on the 18th green of the Sol Classic, sponsor patches gleaming, a home on Aeris Station, a premium driver you own, and three Major titles already in the case — with a fourth one two days away from making you the first human in history to hold them all.

Every credit you earned, every shot you read, every caddie tip, every side-bet, every sponsor you kept and every sponsor you lost — it all led to that stroke.

**That's Intergalactic Golf Tour.**
